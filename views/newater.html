Singapore's National Taps

The 3rd National Tap

The NEWater process recycles our used water into ultra-clean, high-grade reclaimed water, cushioning our water supply against dry weather and moving Singapore towards water sustainability.

During dry periods, NEWater is added to our reservoirs to blend with raw water. The raw water from the reservoir is treated at the waterworks before it is supplied to consumers as tap water.

Singapore Water Story

1961: First water agreement allowing Singapore to draw water from designated areas in Johor for 50 years.

1962: Second water agreement allowing Singapore to draw up to 250 million gallons of water a day from Johor River till 2061.

1970-1980s: The massive clean-up of the Singapore and Kallang Rivers was completed in 1987, ten years after the cleaning of the rivers and its surrounding catchment areas began.
